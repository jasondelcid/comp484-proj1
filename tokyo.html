Tokyo

Big, bright, and surprisingly calm -- a first-timer guide to navigating Tokyo with confidence.

Phrase: ありがとうarigatō (thank you)

Tokyo essentials

Tokyo is a collection of “mini-cities” connected by an excellent rail network. Pick one neighborhood base and everything feels simpler.

Getting around

  • IC card: Suica / PASMO makes transit and convenience stores easy.
  • Rush hour: Avoid 7:30–9:30 AM and 5:30–7:30 PM if possible.
  • Stations: Follow signs and exits carefully—big stations are mini-maze cities.

Etiquette basics

  • Keep voices low on trains.
  • Carry a little cash for smaller shops.
  • Trash bins are rare—bring a small bag.

Quick tips

  • Stand to one side on escalators (varies by area).
  • Learn two lines near your hotel (e.g., Yamanote + one subway).
  • Convenience stores are great for quick breakfast.
